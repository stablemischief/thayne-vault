## SLIDE 2 — THE MARKET

**Agricultural lending is one of the largest underdisrupted credit markets in the United States.**

- U.S. commercial banks: $205 billion in farm loans outstanding (ABA, 2024)
- Farm Credit System: 55 associations, $456.9 billion in loans, 600,000+ borrowers
- 300,000+ new agricultural loans originated per year
- More than half of agricultural lenders only began digitizing operations in 2025
- Farm Credit is consolidating: 69 associations in 2019 → 55 today → projected ~20 — every merger creates a technology replacement window

The buyers are known, the market is concentrated, and the technology infrastructure is a decade behind.

---

## SLIDE 3 — THE PROBLEM

**Processing a farm loan application is a manual, expensive, weeks-long exercise.**

A crop operating line for a farmer with operations across multiple counties requires the analyst to collect, read, and key data from:
FSA-578 acreage reports, Schedule F tax returns, farm financial statements, equipment lists, land lease agreements, crop insurance binders, and entity documents — across farms, counties, and years.

- Estimated analyst time per complex application: **~40 hours**
- Analyst labor cost (fully loaded): **~$40–$44/hour**
- Estimated cost to process one application manually: **~$1,000–$1,200** [*pending Mark Branch validation*]
- One Farm Credit association used **9 separate systems** to originate a single loan *(nCino press release, 2020)*
- GreenStone Farm Credit: closing packages described as **"about an inch and a half thick"** *(DocuSign case study)*

This isn't a workflow problem. It's a data problem. The documents exist. Nobody has built the system that reads them properly.

---

## SLIDE 4 — THE SOLUTION

**AgFin compresses weeks of manual data entry into hours — at $100 per application.**

When documents are uploaded, AgFin:
1. Classifies and validates each document
2. Deploys parallel AI agents — multiple models reading simultaneously
3. Compares outputs field by field, resolves disagreements through arbitration
4. Flags low-confidence fields for analyst review
5. Locks certified data in an immutable, hash-chain audit trail

**What this means for a lender:**
- ~40 hours of analyst work → hours (often under a day)
- $1,000–$1,200 in processing labor → $100 AgFin fee
- **10–12x ROI on labor cost alone** — before accounting for error reduction and turnaround time

**What AgFin does not replace:**
Lien searches, county records verification, FSA good standing checks, insurance confirmation, and title review remain analyst responsibilities. AgFin eliminates the data extraction bottleneck — the largest single time sink in the process.

**Compliance built in from day one:**
- AES-256 PII encryption, row-level security, append-only audit trails
- SOC 2 Type 1 certification in progress → SOC 2 Type 2 pathway underway
- 3 patentable innovations identified — provisional patent filings in progress
