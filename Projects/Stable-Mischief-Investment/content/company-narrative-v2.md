## What Stable Mischief Does

Stable Mischief is an agricultural technology product company. We rapidly build, validate, and launch AI-native software products that help farmers generate and keep more revenue per acre.

Our first product is AgFin — a document intelligence platform for agricultural lenders that replaces weeks of manual data entry with hours of AI-powered extraction. A farmer submits their documents. AgFin reads them, verifies them, resolves conflicts between data points, and delivers a certified, audit-ready file to the analyst. What used to take weeks takes hours.

---

## The Problem

A farmer in Nebraska with 60 farm locations wants to renew his crop operating line. He gathers his FSA records, his Schedule F tax returns, his insurance binders, and his land lease agreements. He drops them off at the bank and waits.

The analyst on the other side opens the first document, keys the data into a spreadsheet, closes it, opens the next one. For a borrower with 12 farm locations across three counties, that's a minimum of 12 FSA forms, multiple insurance policies, tax records, and entity documents. One lender described their closing packages as "about an inch and a half thick." Another runs nine separate systems to originate a single loan. Analysts describe the process as weeks of work.

This is agricultural lending in 2026. The same process, roughly, as 1996.

U.S. commercial banks hold $205 billion in farm loans. The Farm Credit System — the largest agricultural lender — holds $457 billion more across 600,000 borrowers. Federal crop insurance covers $192 billion in liability and processes more than 2.1 million policies per year.

All of it runs on manual data entry and legacy software.

---

## Why Now

Three years ago, this problem didn't have a practical solution. You could digitize the intake form. You could build a portal. But extracting meaning from a farm's actual documents — reading an FSA-578, verifying it against a Schedule F, reconciling conflicting data across a 12-county operation — required a human who understood agriculture.

That is no longer true.

Agentic AI — systems where multiple AI models work in parallel, check each other's outputs, and escalate disagreements for resolution — can now do what a trained analyst does with documents, at the accuracy and traceability that regulated lending requires. The technology is here. The window to build for this market is open. It will not stay open.

## The Technology

AgFin's architecture was built from day one around the requirements of regulated lending, not adapted to fit them afterward.

When documents are uploaded, AgFin classifies them and deploys parallel extraction agents — different AI models reading the same documents simultaneously. Their outputs are compared field by field. An arbitration agent resolves disagreements. Fields below a confidence threshold are flagged for analyst review with documented rationale. When the analyst certifies the file, the data is locked — tamper-evident, hash-verified, compliant.

Every decision, every flag, every resolution is recorded in an immutable audit trail.

This is not a workflow platform that added AI. It is a document intelligence platform built around compliance from the ground up. That distinction is the subject of a provisional patent application filed by Stable Mischief. It is the reason no competitor currently combines parallel multi-agent extraction, field-level arbitration, analyst review workflow, and an immutable compliance audit trail in a single ag-focused platform.

## The Unique Insight

Everyone else approached this problem from the wrong direction.

Existing lending platforms built workflow first — how to route a loan through stages, assign tasks, track status. Then they tried to add document intelligence on top. That creates a fundamental compliance problem: the audit trail is an afterthought, not the foundation.

Stable Mischief built document intelligence first and built workflow around it. The audit trail is baked into the architecture at the field level. Every data point has a provenance. That is what regulators will require. That is what Farm Credit institutions — who operate under strict oversight — will pay for.

The closest architectural parallel is Ocrolus, which built financial document automation for mortgage and consumer lending and is currently valued at over $500 million. No one has done this for agriculture. Until now.

## The Market

The agricultural lending market alone is large enough to build a significant company. It is also the entry point into a much larger adjacent opportunity.

**Agricultural lending:** 300,000+ new ag loans originated every year in the U.S. More than half of ag lenders only began digitizing in 2025.

**Farm Credit System:** 55 associations, technology decisions made at the service corporation level reaching 6–16 associations at once. A concentrated buyer universe with compliance requirements that map directly to what AgFin delivers.

**Crop insurance:** 12 Approved Insurance Providers, 2.1 million policies per year. Every policy generates the same FSA-578s and acreage reports AgFin already processes. The disaster-year backlog — when claims volume spikes 5–10x regionally — is a solved problem with AgFin's architecture.

**SBA lending:** 84,400 loans, $44.8 billion in volume in FY2025. Structured government forms, natural expansion once the ag lending foundation is established.

AgFin's architecture is config-driven. Expanding to a new document type or loan vertical is a configuration change, not a rebuild. The technology scales without starting over.

## The Business Model

AgFin generates revenue on a per-application basis. Lenders pay for each loan application processed through the platform — a straightforward, outcome-based model that scales with their business.

The funding round enables Stable Mischief to mature that model. As AgFin deepens into a full credit solution — moving from document extraction to integrated underwriting intelligence — it becomes an annual license product: predictable revenue for the company, predictable cost for the lender.

A third revenue stream unlocks when AgFin evolves into a farmer-facing lending marketplace: a farmer uploads their documents once and the platform matches them to lenders. That model generates origination-based revenue. It is a fundamentally different scale of opportunity than the lender-facing tool alone.

Kowerk operates on a SaaS model, priced for the small and mid-size ag organizations that will adopt it first.
