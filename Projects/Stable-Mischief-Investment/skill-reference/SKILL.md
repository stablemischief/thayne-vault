## Key Context (Always Relevant)
- **The Company:** Stable Mischief — an AI development firm targeting the agriculture market
- **The Founder:** James Whitfield — product-first, not a traditional developer; strong orchestration + AI tooling skills
- **Key Partner/Advisor:** Chris Johnson — CEO of Agrellus, exits of $4B and $1B; introducing James to institutional fundraising
- **Reference Deck:** Agrellus Investment Opportunity presentation (15 slides, $342B TAM, $5.3M→$113.2M projection)
- **Target Raise:** Likely $8–15M (2–2.5x of actual operational need)
- **Target Investors:** Institutional (Farm Credit, ag banks, insurance companies), strategic partners, VIP early believers (SAFEs)
- **Key Insight from Chris:** Don't think like a bootstrapper. You're selling equity in a company with an exit story, not asking for a loan.

---

## The Two-Tier World (Critical Mental Model)
Chris described two worlds:
1. **Bootstrapper world** — chasing $500K, no salaries, cousin Larry doing dev, no structure. Institutional investors run from this.
2. **Institutional world** — proper corp structure, market-rate salaries in use of funds, SAFE notes, tiered equity, clear exit thesis, team with exits. Institutional investors fund this.

James is making the transition from world 1 to world 2. Every skill in this suite supports that transition.
