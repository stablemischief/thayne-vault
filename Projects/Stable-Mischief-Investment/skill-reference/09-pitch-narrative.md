# Skill 09: Pitch Narrative & Language
## Telling the Story — How to Speak in This World

---

## The Fundamental Narrative Structure
Every great pitch tells a story with this arc:
1. **The world as it is** — what's broken, what's painful, what's being left on the table
2. **Why now** — what changed that makes this the moment
3. **The hero** — why this team is uniquely positioned to solve it
4. **The vision** — what the world looks like when you win
5. **The ask** — what you need from them to make it happen

Everything in your pitch should map to one of these five beats.

## The Stable Mischief Master Narrative (Draft)

**The World As It Is:**
Agriculture is a $342B+ industry running on legacy systems. Loan applications take weeks because analysts manually key data from printed PDFs. Crop insurance forms get processed by "Sue on the green screen computer." Farmers with 150 farm locations have no digital relationship with their lenders. The same transformation that reshaped healthcare and financial services 20–30 years ago has not yet happened in agriculture — and it's overdue.

**Why Now:**
AI has reached a maturity point where what was impossible 3 years ago is buildable today, with a small team, in months. We know because we built it. Agfin — a complex AI underwriting application with multi-agent document processing, bulk FSA handling, and dynamic loan modeling — was built by 2 people in under 6 months. That's the new physics of software development. The window to capture this market before larger players wake up is open. It won't stay open forever.

**The Hero:**
Stable Mischief is an AI-native development company with deep roots in agriculture. We're not consultants who happened to find ag, or ag people who learned to code. We build AI infrastructure — the pick-and-shovel layer — for the agriculture market. Our strategic relationship with Agrellus gives us access to 1.5 million farmers, validated market intelligence, and a proven distribution channel from day one. Our advisors have navigated $5B+ in exits in adjacent markets. We are the team for this moment.

**The Vision:**
A world where every acre in America has a digital relationship — with its lender, its insurer, its input supplier. Where a farmer in Iowa can secure crop financing in hours instead of weeks, because AI-native underwriting has replaced paper and manual data entry. Where insurance companies, banks, and ag input retailers are competing for that farmer's data-backed business. That's the market we're building the infrastructure for.

**The Ask:**
We're raising $X million to scale the team, deploy 3 production products in the first 18 months, and position for $X ARR by end of Year 2. We're not raising to survive — we're raising to win the race.

## Language Framework: What to Say and How to Say It

### Describing the Company
- ✅ "Vertical AI development company"
- ✅ "We build known intelligence for agriculture"
- ✅ "AI-native infrastructure for the ag market"
- ✅ "The technology layer this industry needs and doesn't yet have"
- ❌ "AI startup"
- ❌ "We use AI to..."
- ❌ "We're disrupting agriculture"

### Describing the Technology
- ✅ "Multi-agent AI architecture"
- ✅ "Production-deployed AI underwriting"
- ✅ "AI-native development methodology" (Dynamous)
- ✅ "Known intelligence — trained on domain-specific data"
- ❌ "Chatbot" / "automation" / "machine learning model"

### Describing the Market
- ✅ "Agriculture is where medicine was 30 years ago"
- ✅ "Legacy systems serving a $400B lending market"
- ✅ "1.5 million farmers with no digital lending relationship"
- ✅ "A race to connect the acre to financial services"
- ❌ "Huge market opportunity" (vague)
- ❌ "Everyone in agriculture needs this" (untargeted)

### Describing Traction (Pre-Revenue)
- ✅ "We built a production AI underwriting application in 6 months, 2 people"
- ✅ "Strategic partnership with Agrellus — access to 1.5M farmers from day one"
- ✅ "Two inbound investment interests before we've formally launched a raise"
- ✅ "Advisory team with $5B+ in exits and active ag industry relationships"
- ❌ "We're still building" (don't lead with this)
- ❌ "We're pre-revenue" (don't make this a headline — make it a context note)

### Describing the Ask
- ✅ "We're raising $X million at a $Y cap"
- ✅ "This gets us to $X ARR with 3 deployed products in 18 months"
- ✅ "We're structured for institutional investment from day one"
- ❌ "We need funding to get started" (bootstrap language)
- ❌ "Whatever you're comfortable putting in" (no anchor)

## The "Known Intelligence" (KI) Concept
Chris introduced this framing. Consider it carefully.

**The argument for KI over AI:**
- "AI" is becoming associated with risk, lawsuits, hallucinations, uncertainty
- "Known Intelligence" signals: we've trained this on verified, domain-specific data
- It differentiates you in investor conversations ("we don't just use AI — we build known intelligence for agriculture")
- It's memorable and ownable — no one else is using this language

**How to use it:**
- In the company description: "We build known intelligence for agriculture"
- In the technology description: "Our AI isn't general — it's trained on agriculture-specific data and workflows"
- In investor conversations: "We believe the future of AI in enterprise is vertical and domain-specific — what we call known intelligence"

**Caution:** Test this with Chris and a few investors before committing to it in the deck — it's novel enough that it could either land beautifully or create confusion.

## Handling Common Investor Questions

**"You have no revenue — how do we know this works?"**
"We have something better than revenue — a deployed product. Agfin is live, in use, and solving a problem that has never been solved before in ag finance. We chose to build before we sell because the technology window required it. Revenue follows product. We're now at the product stage."

**"What happens if a big player like John Deere or Salesforce builds this?"**
"That's actually our exit scenario, not our threat. John Deere has tried to build this internally — they haven't. Salesforce doesn't know agriculture. Large companies in adjacent industries consistently acquire rather than build vertical AI. We're building to be acquired."

**"Why you? Why Stable Mischief?"**
"We built a production AI underwriting system in 6 months with 2 people. Our advisor has navigated $5B+ in exits in ag and adjacent markets. We have a distribution partnership with Agrellus and access to 1.5M farmers before we've raised a dollar. The question isn't 'why us' — it's 'who else?'"

**"Isn't agriculture too slow/conservative to adopt this?"**
"Agriculture was slow before AI made it cheap to innovate. The same farmers who were reluctant to adopt GPS autosteer are now using it on every tractor. The key is showing ROI in a language farmers understand — time saved, money saved, access to capital they couldn't get before. We're not selling technology. We're selling faster money."

## The Opening Line
The first 30 seconds sets everything. Options to test:

**Option A (Problem-first):**
"Right now, a farmer in Kansas with 150 farms is waiting 6 weeks for a crop loan decision. The bank analyst is manually keying information from printed PDFs. We built an AI system that does that in minutes. And that's just the beginning of what this market needs."

**Option B (Market-first):**
"Agriculture is a $400 billion lending market that operates the way medicine did in 1990. We are building the technology infrastructure to bring it into the AI era — and we've already proven we can do it."

**Option C (Team-first for warm audiences who know Chris):**
"Chris and I have been working together on Agrellus — the ag-tech platform he co-founded. What we kept seeing was a market that desperately needs AI-native tools and has no one building them properly. So we decided to build the company that does."
