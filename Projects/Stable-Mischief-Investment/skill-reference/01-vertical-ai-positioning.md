# Skill 01: Vertical AI Positioning
## What Kind of Company Is Stable Mischief?

---

## The Core Positioning Problem
Before you can talk valuation, market size, or exit — investors need to understand *what kind of animal this is.* Get this wrong and everything else falls apart.

Stable Mischief is NOT:
- A consulting firm (you don't sell hours)
- A single-product startup (you're not raising for Agfin alone)
- A generic AI company (too broad, too crowded)

Stable Mischief IS:
- A **vertical AI development company** — building productized AI solutions specifically for the agriculture market
- A **picks-and-shovels play** — the infrastructure layer that everyone building in ag needs
- A **platform company** — multiple products share the same underlying AI capabilities, data assets, and distribution relationships

---

## The "Picks and Shovels" Frame
During the California Gold Rush, the people who made the most money weren't the miners — they were the people selling shovels, picks, and jeans (Levi Strauss).

In the AI-in-agriculture gold rush:
- Farmers need tools → Agfin, Kowerk, insurance processors
- Banks need underwriting → Agfin
- Retailers need marketplace data → Agrellus
- **Stable Mischief builds and owns the tools**

This framing is powerful because it means you profit regardless of *which* ag players win. You're not betting on one crop — you're selling the irrigation system.

---

## Vertical AI: Why Agriculture-Specific Is a Feature, Not a Limitation

**General AI companies** (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) are horizontal — they serve everyone, which means they serve no one deeply.

**Vertical AI companies** go deep into one industry:
- They understand the workflows, data formats, regulations, and buyer psychology
- They build proprietary data assets that horizontal companies can't replicate
- They become the industry standard in their niche
- They command premium pricing and higher switching costs
- They are **highly acquirable** by industry incumbents who want the tech but can't build it

Examples of vertical AI companies that have succeeded:
- **Veeva** — pharma-specific CRM/data platform → $40B+ market cap
- **Procore** — construction-specific project management → $10B+
- **Samsara** — fleet/logistics AI → $15B+

Agriculture is the next massive vertical waiting to be captured. Legacy systems, paper-based workflows, massive underserved lending market, 1.5M+ farmers with no digital relationship.

## The Revenue Model Differentiation
Unlike pure SaaS, a vertical AI dev company has multiple monetization angles:

| Revenue Type | Example | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| **Licensing** | AI underwriting engine licensed to banks | High margin, recurring, scales without headcount |
| **White-label** | Agfin platform deployed under a bank's brand | Large contracts, sticky |
| **SaaS** | Kowerk subscriptions | Predictable ARR |
| **Usage-based** | Per-document processed, per-loan underwritten | Scales with customer success |
| **Build fees + equity** | Build a tool for a client, take equity instead of full cash | Aligns incentives, builds portfolio |
| **Data monetization** | Aggregated farm data sold upstream to manufacturers/insurers | Compounding value over time |

The investor story: **we don't just build software, we accumulate strategic data assets and recurring revenue streams simultaneously.**

## The "Proof of Concept" Argument (Agfin as Evidence)
You don't have 10,000 users. But you have something more powerful to a sophisticated investor:

**You built a complex, novel AI product (Agfin) in under 6 months, with 2 people, using AI-native development methodology.**

That is your proof of execution. The pitch isn't "look at our revenue" — it's "look at what we can build, how fast, and how cheaply. Now imagine what we do with capital."

Specific evidence points to highlight:
- Multi-agent document processing architecture (Claude + Gemini QA loop)
- Bulk FSA upload and AI-verified document ingestion
- Entity/farm/tract data model built to serve 8 profile types
- Built while simultaneously advising on Agrellus Agfin product
