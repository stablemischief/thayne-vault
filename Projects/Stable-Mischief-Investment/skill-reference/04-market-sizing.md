# Skill 04: Market Sizing & Opportunity Framing
## TAM / SAM / SOM and How to Frame a Market Without a Direct Competitor

---

## The Three Market Numbers Every Deck Needs

**TAM — Total Addressable Market**
The total revenue opportunity if you captured 100% of the market.
This is the universe. It makes investors understand the size of the prize.

**SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market**
The portion of TAM you can realistically reach with your business model and distribution.
This is your real playing field today.

**SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market**
The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in the near term (3–5 years).
This is your actual projection target. It should be credible, not fantasy.

## How to Research and Build Your Market Numbers

**Primary sources to cite:**
- USDA Economic Research Service (ERS) — farm count, lending volumes, input spending
- FDIC ag loan data — total ag lending in US
- McKinsey/Deloitte ag-tech reports
- Pitchbook / CB Insights ag-tech funding reports
- Agrellus's own deck numbers (you have insider access — use them as a comp/reference)

**The formula approach:**
TAM = (# of potential customers) × (average revenue per customer per year)

Example for Stable Mischief licensing play:
- US banks and credit unions with ag loan portfolios: ~2,500+
- Average annual licensing fee for AI underwriting platform: $50K–$200K/year
- SAM: 500 institutions × $100K = $50M/year in licensing alone (just one revenue stream)

## The No-Competitor Problem (And How to Solve It)

Chris called this Agrellus's hardest slide: "People are like, well I need a competitor. If you don't have a competitor, you don't have a market."

**The trap:** Investors have been trained that no competitor = no market.
**The truth:** In vertical AI, *you're often the first mover at the intersection of two existing markets.*

**The framing solution:**
Don't say "we have no competitors." Say:
"We sit at the intersection of [ag-tech software], [AI/ML platforms], and [financial services technology]. Each of those markets has players. No one has combined them the way we have for this specific vertical."

Then show a 2x2 or comparison matrix:
- Column headers: your key capabilities
- Rows: existing players (Granular, Trimble Ag, John Deere Ops Center, AgriWebb, traditional ag software)
- Check marks where they have it, X where they don't
- You have all the checks

**The narrative:** "Our competitors may offer one or two pieces of the puzzle. We're building the only platform that delivers the full picture, purpose-built for AI-native agriculture operations."

## The "Agriculture = Medicine 30 Years Ago" Frame
Chris used this comparison. It's powerful. Here's how to deploy it:

- Medical in the 1990s: paper records, siloed data, manual billing, no digital workflow
- Healthcare tech investment followed: Epic, Cerner, Veeva, Change Healthcare — each worth billions
- Agriculture TODAY is where medicine was in 1995
- The difference: we have AI now. The transformation will happen 5–10x faster

"We're not waiting for agriculture to be disrupted. We're the disruption."

## The Geography Expansion Angle
Agrellus deck: targeting North America first, then Australia, Brazil, South Africa, Europe, Argentina in 2026+.

Stable Mischief can use the same expansion story:
- Phase 1: US agriculture market (the largest, most accessible)
- Phase 2: Canada, Australia (English-speaking, similar ag financing models)
- Phase 3: Brazil, South America (massive ag market, underserved)

This turns a US-only story into a global opportunity without overclaiming.

## The "Race" Frame (Chris's Language)
"I think it's a race. To win the race, you have to go fast, accurate, deliberate."

Use this in the pitch:
- Agriculture is a race to connect the acre — to the bank, the insurer, the input retailer
- Legacy players are slow because they're built on legacy systems
- We are AI-native from day one — our build speed *is* our competitive advantage
- Every month we're not funded is a month someone else could be running

This creates urgency without desperation. It's honest — it is a race.
