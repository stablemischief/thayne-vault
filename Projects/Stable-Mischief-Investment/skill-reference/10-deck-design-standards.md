# Skill 10: Deck Design & Presentation Standards
## What Institutional-Quality Decks Look Like

---

## The Signal Value of Design
Institutional investors see hundreds of decks. Design quality is a signal:
- **Polished, professional** → "This team sweats the details. They'll treat our capital the same way."
- **Sloppy, inconsistent** → "If they can't build a clean deck, what does their product look like?"

You are not just communicating information — you are communicating competence and credibility.

---

## Format Specifications

**Software:** PowerPoint, Keynote, or Google Slides (all acceptable). Keynote tends to produce cleaner output on Mac. Export to PDF for distribution.

**Dimensions:** 16:9 widescreen (1920×1080 or similar)

**Length:** 12–18 slides for full pitch deck. Never more than 20. Investors' attention is finite.

**Font:**
- Headline: Clean sans-serif — Inter, Helvetica Neue, DM Sans, or similar
- Body: Same family, lighter weight
- Never Comic Sans, Times New Roman, or decorative fonts
- Minimum body size: 18pt
- Headlines: 28–40pt

**Colors:**
- 1–2 primary brand colors + neutral (white/light gray backgrounds)
- Stable Mischief brand colors — reference `stable-mischief-skills/brand-identity.md` for exact values
- Do NOT use default PowerPoint/Google Slides color schemes

**Images:**
- Use high-quality stock photography for scene-setting (Unsplash, Pexels — free)
- Actual product screenshots where relevant
- Avoid clip art and generic business imagery

---

## Slide Density Rules

**The "5-5-5" Rule (modified):**
- Maximum 5 bullet points per slide
- Maximum 5 words per bullet point (the bullet is a cue for you to speak, not a paragraph)
- Maximum 5 slides without a visual break (chart, image, diagram)

**One message per slide.** If you can't answer "what is the one thing I want them to remember from this slide?" — cut or split.

**Numbers over words.** "$342B TAM" > "There is a very large total addressable market." Numbers are concrete and memorable.

**Visuals over bullets wherever possible:**
- Market size → concentric circles chart
- Revenue model → visual ecosystem diagram
- Financial projections → bar/line chart
- Team → photo grid with credentials
- Competitive landscape → comparison matrix

---

## The Information Hierarchy

Every slide should have:
1. **Headline** (top of slide) — the conclusion, not the topic. "Agriculture Is Where Medicine Was 30 Years Ago" not "Market Context"
2. **Supporting evidence** (body) — 3–5 bullets or a visual that proves the headline
3. **Source/attribution** (footnote) — for any data points

**Bad headline:** "Market Opportunity"
**Good headline:** "$342B Market With No Dominant AI Player — Yet"

**Bad headline:** "Our Team"
**Good headline:** "Builders and Operators With $5B+ in Exits Behind Them"

## Three Decks You Actually Need

**1. The Email Teaser (1–2 pages)**
- PDF, attached to initial intro email
- Covers: who you are, what you build, the market, the ask
- Goal: get a meeting
- Design: as clean and sharp as the full deck

**2. The Pitch Deck (12–18 slides)**
- Used in meetings — you present it
- Has minimal text, lots of visuals
- Your narrative fills the gaps
- Leave a PDF copy at the end of meetings

**3. The Data Room (shared folder)**
- Full financials and assumptions
- Cap table
- Corporate structure / legal docs (once formed)
- Product screenshots / demo materials
- Team bios and LinkedIn profiles
- Press / social proof if applicable
- Agrellus partnership documentation

## Presentation Delivery Standards

**In person:**
- Stand (if possible) — commands more authority
- Don't read the slides — use them as anchors
- Make eye contact with the primary decision-maker
- Have a printed copy in front of you with speaker notes

**Remote (Zoom/Teams):**
- Use slide share + camera on (face visible)
- Good lighting — ring light or window light
- Clean background or branded virtual background
- Have backup: if screen share fails, email the deck ahead of time

**Timing:**
- Full pitch deck: 20–30 minutes
- Leave 20–30 minutes for Q&A
- If they interrupt with questions early — welcome it, don't push them back to "let me finish the deck"

## Tools for Building the Deck

**Design tools:**
- **Canva** — easiest, has good pitch deck templates, acceptable output
- **Beautiful.ai** — AI-assisted deck building, very clean output
- **Pitch.com** — startup-focused, collaboration features, good design
- **Keynote (Mac)** — most control, cleanest PDF export
- **Google Slides** — easiest to share/collaborate, less design control

**Recommended for James given resources:**
Start with **Canva** or **Beautiful.ai** — fast, professional, doesn't require a designer. Once you have a content draft approved by Chris, consider having a professional designer do a final polish pass ($500–$2,000 on Fiverr or 99designs for a deck redesign).

## The Cover Email (When Sending the Deck)
Your deck is only as good as the email that introduces it. Template:

**Subject:** Stable Mischief — AI Infrastructure for Agriculture [Introduction via Chris Johnson]

"[Name],

[Chris Johnson / mutual contact] suggested I reach out. We're building the AI infrastructure layer for agricultural finance and data — the vertical technology platform that agriculture has been missing.

I've attached a brief overview. Happy to walk you through it on a 20-minute call.

[James]"

**One paragraph. Attached deck. Clear ask (a call).** Nothing more.
