# Skill 08: Exit Strategy Framing
## Who Buys You, For How Much, and Why — Told Before They Ask

---

## Why Exit Strategy Is Slide 1 (In Their Mind)
Chris said it directly: "The main thing an investor wants to know is what's my exit. That's all they want to know."

The entire pitch is working backwards from an exit. Every other slide — market size, team, product — exists to make the exit story more credible.

**Lead with the exit thesis, not the product.**
Most founders bury the exit or treat it like an afterthought. Sophisticated investors respect founders who have thought this through from day one.

---

## The Exit Landscape for Stable Mischief

### Exit Path 1: Strategic Acquisition (Most Likely, Most Attractive)
**Who acquires you:**
- **Farm Credit Banks / CoBank** — The Farm Credit System has $400B+ in lending. They want AI underwriting. They can't build it. They'll buy it.
- **Insurance Companies (Crop Insurers)** — RCIS, Crop Risk Services, ProAg. They process thousands of farm applications manually. Your AI underwriting engine is worth billions to them in efficiency gains.
- **Agricultural Banks** — Rabo AgriFinance, American AgCredit. They serve farmers, they compete on speed of service. AI underwriting is a competitive weapon.
- **John Deere** — Chris specifically mentioned this. Deere has transformed from equipment company to ag data company. They spend billions acquiring tech. Your platform fits their "connected farm" thesis.
- **Trimble** — Ag software company ($3B+ market cap). Actively acquires ag-tech.
- **AGCO / CNH Industrial** — Equipment companies expanding into digital ag
- **Bayer / Corteva** — Ag input companies want farmer relationships and data

**Why they acquire vs. build:**
- Speed — building this takes years
- Risk — they don't know how to do AI-native development
- Distribution — they have the farmers, you have the technology
- Data assets — your accumulated farm data is irreplaceable

**Comparable acquisitions:**
- Granular (farm management software) → acquired by Corteva for ~$300M (2017)
- Conservis (farm management) → acquired by Rabo AgriFinance
- Farmers Edge → raised $58M+ before going public
- Solinftec → raised significant rounds from ag strategics
- AgriWebb → acquired by Nutrien for ~$130M

**Target acquisition range for Stable Mischief:** $100M–$500M+ at Year 5–7, depending on ARR and strategic value.

### Exit Path 2: Private Equity Rollup
- PE firms are actively rolling up ag-tech companies into platforms
- Less glamorous than strategic acquisition but very real
- Typically pays 4–8x ARR for software companies
- At $25M ARR (Year 5 target), that's $100M–$200M valuation

### Exit Path 3: Series A → Growth Equity → IPO (Less Likely Near-Term)
- Requires more traction before this path opens
- Ag-tech IPOs have had mixed results (Farmers Edge went public and struggled)
- More likely path: Series A to attract growth capital, then strategic acquisition

## How to Frame the Exit in the Pitch

**The one-sentence exit story:**
"We are building the AI infrastructure layer for agricultural finance and data. Our exit is acquisition by a financial institution, insurance company, or strategic ag player who wants to own this capability rather than compete against it — at a target valuation of $150M–$500M within 5–7 years."

**The expanded narrative (for investor Q&A):**
"Agriculture is a $400B+ lending market that hasn't been touched by modern AI. The institutions serving this market — Farm Credit banks, crop insurers, ag lenders — are actively looking for technology they can either acquire or license. We're building to be the obvious choice. Chris Johnson, who is our lead advisor, has navigated these exact institutional relationships through his time at Agrellus. We're not building in a vacuum."

---

## Return Math for Investors (Show You've Done This)
Investors need to see that the math works for them.

**Example at $8M raise with $50M valuation cap (SAFE):**

If you exit at $200M:
- 16% of $200M = $32M return on $8M invested = **4x**

If you exit at $500M:
- 16% of $500M = $80M return on $8M invested = **10x**

If your SAFE cap is $40M (Tier 1 investors):
- Their effective ownership is higher → better returns
- At $200M exit: could be 20% → $40M → **5x**
- At $500M exit: $100M → **12.5x**

**You don't need to show all this math in the deck** — but you should understand it, and be ready to walk through it if asked.

## The "Who Would Buy This" Conversation
When Chris said he could think of buyers "in a heartbeat" — he was signaling he has those relationships.

**Your job:** Know the list, name names, explain the logic.
"Farm Credit System has stated publicly they want to invest in ag-tech. CoBank specifically has a venture arm. Insurance companies processing 2M+ farm applications a year would save hundreds of millions in underwriting costs with our platform. Those are our acquisition targets."

---

## Timing the Exit Conversation
- Don't lead with the exit in the first 30 seconds — let them get excited about the opportunity first
- Do have a crisp exit answer ready because they will ask
- Frame it as confidence, not a plan to abandon the company: "We're building something valuable — and valuable things get acquired by people who need them."
