Getting Started with Affirm

Everything you need to know before you open the app for the first time.

Affirm isn't a generic wellness app. Every affirmation, teaching, future memory exercise, and alignment response it generates is written specifically for you — grounded in your own inner work, your own focus areas, and the specific patterns you're in the process of rewiring.

Before any of that is possible, the app needs to understand who you are and what you're working on. That's what the setup conversation is for — and it's worth doing with intention.

This guide walks you through exactly what to expect, why it matters, and how to get the most out of it.

The Setup Process
1
You'll receive a personal invite

Affirm is invite-only. You'll receive an email with a unique invite link. Tap it, set a password, and you're in. No public sign-up, no waitlist queue — just a direct path in.

✦ Your invite link is single-use. Once your account is created, return to the app by going directly to the login screen.
2
A short conversation builds your Blueprint

The first thing you'll do is a brief conversational setup — about 10 to 12 questions. It's not a form or a questionnaire. It's a real dialogue. The AI will ask you one question at a time, listen to your answers, and build a deep personal profile called your Blueprint as you go.

This conversation takes about 5 to 10 minutes. You can stop at any point and come back — your progress is saved automatically.

✦ The more honestly and openly you engage with this conversation, the more powerfully the app will speak to you. There are no right or wrong answers — only yours.
3
Everything becomes personal to you

Once your Blueprint is built, the app uses it for everything. Every affirmation it generates. Every Joe teaching it serves you. Every future memory it writes. Every energy center diagnosis. All of it pulls directly from your specific work areas, your limiting blueprints, your future memories, and your elevated emotion anchors.

Nothing is pulled from a static list. Everything is written fresh for you, every time.

What Your Blueprint Contains

As you move through the setup conversation, the AI builds a personal profile across six dimensions. Here's what each one is — and an example of what it might look like for a real user.

Work Areas
What you're actively working on becoming
e.g., Financial abundance, Building authentic relationships, Reclaiming identity after major life change
Limiting Blueprints
The subconscious patterns you're in the process of rewiring
e.g., "I am not enough," Scarcity programming, Fear of abandonment, Broadcasting 'need' instead of abundance
Future Memories
The specific visions from your meditation practice — written as already-lived memories
e.g., The morning I woke knowing the business was fully funded. Walking onto the beach, completely free.
Elevated Emotion Anchors
The elevated states you most want to live in and broadcast
e.g., Gratitude, Freedom, Wholeness, Love, Peace, Abundance
Energy Center Focus
The centers you're most actively working to open or clear
e.g., Heart (4), Throat (5), Solar Plexus (3)
Additional Context
Anything else that shapes your current practice and life situation
e.g., Attending 10-day retreat in June. Recently emerged from long-term relationship. Grieving a significant loss.

When you imagine your future self who has fully stepped into financial freedom, what does that person feel like in their body? Not what they have, but who they are.
Calm. Not chasing anything. Like there's nothing to prove. I can just… be. That feeling of peace and freedom — that's what I'm after more than the money itself.
Peace and freedom as the signal, not money as the goal — that's exactly how Joe frames it. The field responds to the feeling of already having, not the striving. I'm noting calm, peace, and freedom as your primary elevated emotion anchors. Now — is there a specific moment or vision from your meditations that represents this future self? Something you've already "remembered" in the field?

Common Questions

What if I stop mid-conversation and come back later?
Your conversation is saved automatically after every message. If you close the app or your phone goes to sleep, you'll pick up exactly where you left off the next time you open Affirm. Nothing is lost.
What if my answers change over time?
They will — and that's expected. As your inner work deepens, you can update your Blueprint through the Settings page. There's a conversational "Reflect & Update" flow that lets you share what's shifted, and the AI will propose specific updates to your Blueprint before anything changes.
How much do I need to share?
The app works with whatever you give it — but the more specific and honest you are, the more powerfully it will speak to you. A generic blueprint produces generic content. A deeply personal blueprint produces content that stops you mid-breath. It's worth going deep.
Is this a therapy tool?
No. Affirm is a personal alignment tool — it helps you return to elevated emotional states and stay connected to your blueprint throughout the day. It applies Dr. Joe Dispenza's and Florence Scovel Shinn's frameworks to your specific work. It does not provide medical or psychological care.
Who sees my information?
Only you. Your Blueprint and conversations are stored privately in a SOC 2 Type 2 certified database. Your data is never used to train AI models. Invite-only access means no strangers can ever reach your account.
