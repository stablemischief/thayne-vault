# Long Langstroth Hive — Build Plans

**Source:** https://horizontalhive.com/how-to-build/long-langstroth-plans.shtml
**Saved:** 2026-04-24
**Standard config:** 31 deep frames

## Overview

Horizontal hive accepting standard deep Langstroth frames. Under $50 in materials, ~2 hour build with basic tools. Thick 1.5" walls (2×12 lumber) for durability and insulation.

|---|---|---|

| Strip-2-Long (rim c) | 48" × 1-3/4" | **75-1/2" × 1-3/4"** |
| Short rim strips | unchanged | unchanged |

### 51-Frame Build Notes
- **Long walls at 72-5/16"** — a single 12' 2×12 won't yield two long walls. You'll need **two 8' boards** (or one 12' + scraps).
- **Plywood top at 76-1/2"** — exceeds a 4'×8' sheet length (96"). Still fits, but you'll need to orient the cut carefully along the 8' dimension. Alternatively, do a two-piece top with an overlap joint.
- **Bottom at 73-11/16"** — same consideration, but fits within 96".
- **Floor sag risk** — at 6+ feet, add a center cross-brace or use 3/4" plywood for the bottom.
- **Weight** — fully loaded could exceed 250 lbs. Ensure stand/legs can handle it. Consider a center leg pair or continuous rail support.
- **Entrances** — scale from 3 to 5 entrances, maintaining the 6" wide × 1/2" tall spec with 2" end offset. Space remaining entrances evenly.

## Assembly Instructions

### Step 1: Cut Walls
Cut 2×12 into four wall pieces. Mark outside-facing surfaces — choose good-looking sides with proper grain orientation.

### Step 2: Verify Width Uniformity
Check all four pieces have uniform width at both ends. 2×12 boards may vary up to 1/8". If inconsistent, run all through table saw to achieve uniform width (e.g., 11-1/8").

### Step 3: Cut Frame Rests (Rabbet)
Cut rabbet **3/8" wide × 3/4" deep** along the top inner edge of both long walls. This is the frame rest.

### Step 4: Cut Entrances
Cut three entrances in the **front** long wall:
- Each entrance: **6" wide × 1/2" tall**
- Positioned **1-1/4" from bottom edge**
- **2" from each end**, one centered
- Method: Drill 1/2" hole, then use router with 1/2" straight bit (jigsaw works too)

### Step 5: Assemble Box
Assemble on flat surface, bottom facing up, rabbeted frame rests facing inward. **Long walls go between end walls.** Use right-angle clamps at all four corners. Secure with **three 3-1/2" screws per corner**. Drill pilot holes to prevent cracking.

### Step 6: Cut Bottom Rabbet
Cut rabbet **3/4" wide × 1/2" deep** along entire inner perimeter for bottom panel insertion.
- **Alternative:** Skip rabbet entirely — screw bottom on directly. If so, reduce wall width to 10-3/4" in Step 2.

### Step 7: Cut Plywood
Cut all plywood pieces per cut list above.

### Step 8: Attach Bottom
Insert bottom piece and attach with **1-1/8" deck screws**. Do NOT use glue — keep removable for future maintenance.

### Step 9: Cut Legs
Cut 2×4 with 45° angle cuts. Keep 1-1/2" (narrow) side facing up. Measure 30-1/2" on both ends, make angle cuts. Four legs per hive.

### Step 10: Attach Legs
Turn hive on side. Mark 2" from top edge on end wall. Position leg and attach with **two 3-1/2" screws + one 2-1/2" screw**. Repeat all four corners.

### Step 11: Make Landing Boards
Tilt table saw to 15°, rip landing board strips to ~1-11/16" with angled edge. Cut into six 7-1/2" pieces. (Alternative: use larger scrap boards up to 10" × 3-1/2".)

### Step 12: Attach Landing Boards
Center under each entrance. Attach with **two 1-1/8" screws** (one per side of entrance). Pre-drill to prevent splitting.

### Step 13: Assemble Top Rim
Two top configurations available:
- **Config 1:** 1-1/4" rim — short strips between long strips
- **Config 2:** 1-3/4" rim — long strips between short strips

Clamp at right angles, pre-drill, connect with 1-1/8" screws (1–2 per corner).

### Step 14: Attach Top Panel
Apply wood glue to rim. Attach top plywood panel with pneumatic staple gun (1" staples) or pre-drilled 1-1/8" screws.

### Step 15: Insulate Top
Cut insulation board to **48" × 21-5/8"** (for 31-frame; scale for 51-frame: ~75-1/2" × 21-5/8"). Insert into top — should fit tightly. Secure with construction glue if needed.

### Step 16: Paint
Apply two coats primer/sealer, then one coat light-colored exterior paint to ALL exterior surfaces. Pay special attention to exposed plywood edges.

## Tips & Notes

- **Wind protection:** Put a rock or concrete block on top so it doesn't blow off. If heavy, position over a wall edge (not center) to avoid pushing through the top.
- **Tilt:** Install hive at slight **1° forward incline** for rainwater runoff from entrances.
- **Burlap trick:** Cover frames with burlap layer to avoid disturbing bees when opening the top.
- **Aluminum flashing:** Extend top life by covering with aluminum flashing — 24" wide × 50-7/16" long (scale for 51-frame). Lay flat, center top, score perimeter, cut corners, fold flaps, secure with manual staple gun using 3/8" staples.
- **Hinges:** Optional — install hinges on top for ease of opening.
- **Gable roof:** Optional modification for aesthetics and added insulation.
- **Screen bottom board:** Optional — with oil trays for mite/beetle management.

## Tools Required

- Circular saw with guide (or hand saw)
- Sliding miter saw (or hand saw)
- Table saw (or router with 3/4" straight bit)
- Cordless drill with pilot bit, 1/2" bit, and driver bits
- Router with 3/4" and 1/2" straight bits
- Four right-angle clamps (e.g., Bessey WS-3)
